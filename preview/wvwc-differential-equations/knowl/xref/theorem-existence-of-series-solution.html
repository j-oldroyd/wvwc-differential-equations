Theorem 5.3.2. Existence of Series Solution.

Suppose that \(a\) is an ordinary point of the differential equation \(y''+P(x)y^\prime+Q(x)y=0\text{.}\) Then the ODE has two linearly independent solutions of the form
\begin{equation*} y(x) = \sum_{n=0}^{\infty}c_{n}(x-a)^{n}\text{.} \end{equation*}
The radius of convergence of the resulting solution is at least as large as the distance from \(a\) to the nearest singular point of the ODE.
