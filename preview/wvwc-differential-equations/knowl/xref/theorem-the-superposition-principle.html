Theorem 2.1.6. The superposition principle.

Let \(y_{1}\) and \(y_{2}\) denote two (possibly different) solutions of the ODE \(y''+P(x)y^\prime+Q(x)y = 0\text{.}\) Then any linear combination of these solutions is itself another solution of the same ODE.

Proof.

We need to show that \(y = c_{1}y_{1} + c_{2}y_{2}\) is another solution of the same ODE, where \(c_{1},c_{2}\) are arbitrary constants. To do this, weâ€™ll just plug the linear combination into the ODE and simplify:
\begin{align*} y''+P(x)y^\prime+Q(x)y & = (c_{1}y_{1}+c_{2}y_{2})''+P(x)(c_{1}y_{1}+c_{2}y_{2})+Q(x)(c_{1}y_{1}+c_{2}y_{2})y \\ & = c_{1}y''_{1}+c_{2}y''_{2} + c_{1}P(x)y'_{1}+c_{2}P(x)y'_{2} + c_{1}Q(x)y_{1} + c_{2}Q(x)y_{2}\\ & = c_{1}(y''_{1}+P(x)y'_{1}+Q(x)y_{1}) + c_{2}(y''_{2}+P(x)y'_{2}+Q(x)y_{2}) \\ & = 0. \end{align*}
