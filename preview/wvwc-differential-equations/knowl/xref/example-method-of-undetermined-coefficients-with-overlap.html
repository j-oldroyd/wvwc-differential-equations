Example 2.4.4. Method of undetermined coefficients with overlap.

Find the solution \(y(x)\) of the IVP
\begin{equation*} y''+9y = 3\cos(3x),\quad y(0) = 1,y^\prime(0) = -1 \end{equation*}
Solution.
We can start this example the same way we’ve done the previous ones. First, we find \(y_{c}\) by solving \(y''+9y=0\text{.}\) So
\begin{equation*} y_{c} = c_{1}\cos(3x)+c_{2}\sin(3x). \end{equation*}
Now we find \(y_{p}\text{.}\) Since the RHS of the ODE is \(3\cos(3x)\text{,}\) we’ll guess that \(y_{p} = A\cos(3x)+B\sin(3x)\text{.}\) However, this will cause us problems! Since \(\cos(3x),\sin(3x)\) are both solutions of the corresponding homogeneous ODE, then plugging \(y_{p}\) into \(y''+9y\) will just give \(0\) again, instead of \(3\cos(3x)\text{.}\)
The problem here is that our guess for \(y_{p}\) overlaps with \(y_{c}\text{.}\) To fix this, we’ll multiply our guess for \(y_{p}\) by the smallest power of \(x\) that removes the overlap. In this case, we’ll just multiply by \(x\) to get
\begin{equation*} y_{p} = x[A\cos(3x)+B\sin(3x)] \end{equation*}
Now, we’ll plug our modified guess into the ODE and set it equal to \(3\cos(3x)\text{:}\)
\begin{align*} 3\cos(3x)& = y_{p}'' + 9y\\ & = -6A\sin(3x)+6B\cos(3x) \end{align*}
So we need \(-6A = 0\) and \(6B = 3\text{,}\) or just \(A = 0,B = \frac{1}{2}\text{.}\) Hence
\begin{equation*} y_{p} = x\left[\frac{1}{2}\sin(3x)\right], \end{equation*}
and the general solution is then
\begin{equation*} y = y_{c}+y_{p} = c_{1}\cos(3x)+c_{2}\sin(3x) + \frac{x}{2}\sin(3x). \end{equation*}
To find the solution of the IVP, we just plug in the initial conditions. Since \(y(0) = 1\text{,}\) this gives
\begin{equation*} 1 = c_{1}. \end{equation*}
And since
\begin{equation*} y^\prime = -3c_{1}\sin(3x)+3c_{2}\cos(3x) + \frac{1}{2}\sin(3x)+\frac{3}{2}x\cos(3x), \end{equation*}
\(y^\prime(0)=-1\) gives
\begin{equation*} -1 = 3c_{2}\Rightarrow c_{2} = -\frac{1}{3}. \end{equation*}
So
\begin{equation*} y = \cos(3x)-\frac{1}{3}\sin(3x)+\frac{x}{2}\sin(3x). \end{equation*}
