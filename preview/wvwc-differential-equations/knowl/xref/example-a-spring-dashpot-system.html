Example 2.3.4. A spring-dashpot system.

Suppose that an object of mass \(m = 25\) is attached to both a spring and a dashpot. The mass is held \(1\) meter to the left of the springâ€™s equilibrium position \(x = 0\text{.}\) The force of the spring on the mass is \(F_{S} = -226x\) and the force of the dashpot on the mass is \(F_{R} = -10x^\prime\text{,}\) where \(x\) is the displacement of the mass. At time \(t = 0\text{,}\) the mass is released. Find \(x(t)\text{.}\)
Solution.
The ODE that models the motion of this mass is
\begin{equation*} 25x'' + 10x^\prime + 226x = 0, \end{equation*}
and the roots of the corresponding characteristic equation are
\begin{equation*} r_{1} = -\frac{1}{5} \pm \frac{1}{5}\sqrt{-225} = -\frac{1}{5}\pm3i. \end{equation*}
We can already see that the motion must be underdamped since we have complex roots, and the position \(x(t)\) itself is given by
\begin{equation*} x(t) = e^{-\frac{t}{5}}(A\cos3t + B\sin3t). \end{equation*}
Now we can use the initial conditions \(x(0) = -1\) and \(x^\prime(0) = 0\) to find \(A\) and \(B\text{.}\) Doing so, we quickly get \(A = -1\) and \(B = -\frac{1}{15}\text{.}\) Hence
\begin{equation*} x(t) = -e^{-\frac{t}{5}}\left(\cos3t + \frac{1}{15}\sin3t\right). \end{equation*}
