Example 4.1.6. Computing a matrix-vector product.

Let \(A = \begin{bmatrix} 1\amp0\\-3\amp2 \end{bmatrix},\vec{v}_{1} = \begin{bmatrix}1\\1\end{bmatrix}\) and \(\vec{v}_{2} = \begin{bmatrix}0\\5\end{bmatrix}\text{.}\) Compute \(A\vec{v}_{1}\) and \(A\vec{v}_{2}\text{.}\)
Solution.
By definition,
\begin{equation*} A\vec{v}_{1} = \begin{bmatrix}1\cdot1+0\cdot1\\-3\cdot1+2\cdot1\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}1\\-1\end{bmatrix}\quad\text{and}\quad A\vec{v}_{2} = \begin{bmatrix}1\cdot0+0\cdot5\\-3\cdot0+2\cdot5\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}0\\10\end{bmatrix}. \end{equation*}
