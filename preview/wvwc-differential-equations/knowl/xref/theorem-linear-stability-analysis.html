Suppose \(\dv{x}{t} = f(x)\) where \(f(x)\) is continuously differentiable, and let \(x^{*}\) denote a critical point/equilibrium solution of the ODE. If \(f^\prime (x^{*}) \lt 0\text{,}\) then \(x^{*}\) is stable and solutions near \(x^{*}\) will move exponentially towards \(x^{*}\text{.}\) If \(f^\prime (x^{*}) \gt 0\text{,}\) then \(x^{*}\) is unstable and solutions near \(x^{*}\) will move exponentially away from \(x^{*}\text{.}\) If \(f^\prime (x^{*}) = 0\text{,}\) then more advanced methods are required.
