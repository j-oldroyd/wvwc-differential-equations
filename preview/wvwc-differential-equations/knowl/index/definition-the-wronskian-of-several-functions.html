======= Definition 3.1.1. The Wronskian of several functions.

Let \(\{y_{1},\dots,y_{n}\}\) be a collection of functions. Then the Wronskian of \(\{y_{1},\dots,y_{n}\}\text{,}\) denoted by \(W(y_{1},\dots,y_{n})\text{,}\) is defined by
\begin{equation*} W(y_{1},\dots,y_{n}) = \left|\begin{array}{cccc} y_{1} \amp y_{2} \amp \dots \amp y_{n} \\ y^\prime_{1} \amp y^\prime_{2} \amp \dots \amp y^\prime_{n} \\ \vdots \amp \vdots \amp \ddots \amp \vdots \\ y^{(n-1)}_{1} \amp y^{(n-1)}_{2} \amp \dots \amp y^{(n-1)}_{n} \\ \end{array}\right|. \end{equation*}
