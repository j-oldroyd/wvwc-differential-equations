Example 2.2.4. Spring-mass system revisited.

An object of mass 4 kg is attached to a horizontal, frictionless spring. Suppose the spring constant is given by \(k=16\text{.}\) The mass is held 3 m to the right of the spring’s equilibrium position, and is then released at time \(t=0\) where \(t\) is in seconds. Find the displacement \(x(t)\) of the mass.
Solution.
We know from Example 2.1.4 that the second-order ODE given by
\begin{equation*} 4x'' + kx = 0\quad\text{or}\quad x'' + 4x = 0 \end{equation*}
provides a model for \(x(t)\text{,}\) but now we are in a position to solve it. The characteristic equation of this ODE is \(r^{2} + 4 = 0\text{,}\) which has roots \(r=\pm2i\text{.}\) The imaginary roots are not a problem, and in fact provide significant information about the motion of the mass, as we’ll soon see. The general solution of the ODE is
\begin{equation*} x = c_{1}e^{-2it} + c_{2}e^{2it}. \end{equation*}
The initial conditions are \(x(0) = 3\) and \(x^\prime(0) = 0\text{,}\) which give the equations
\begin{align*} 3 & = c_{1} + c_{2} \\ 0 & = -2ic_{1} + 2ic_{2} \end{align*}
The second equation implies that \(c_{1} = c_{2}\text{,}\) and applying this to the first equation now gives \(c_{1} = c_{2} = \frac{3}{2}\text{.}\) Hence the displacement of the mass is given by
\begin{equation*} x = \frac{3}{2}e^{-2it} + \frac{3}{2}e^{2it}. \end{equation*}
